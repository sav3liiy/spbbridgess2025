Tuchkov bridge

Tuchkov Bridge is a drawbridge that connects Bolshoy Prospekt on the Petrogradskaya side with two lines of Vasilyevsky Island (Kadetskaya and Pervaya). It was built in the middle of the XVIII century and was originally called the Nikolsky Bridge. In 1835, the bridge was reconstructed and received a movable central span. In 1965, the bridge was renovated and acquired a modern look.

Tuchkov Bridge was built in 1965 according to the design of architects V. V. Demchenko and B. B. Levin. Its length is 226 meters and its width is 36 meters, of which 28 meters is occupied by the roadway. The sidewalk of the bridge is quite wide, up to 4 meters. The bridge is a reinforced concrete structure consisting of cantilever-girder spans. In the center of the river, the bridge has a double drawbridge with a total length of 50 meters. The bridge's opening angle is 69 degrees.
