Blagoveshchensky Bridge is an architectural monument located in St. Petersburg. It was built in 1842-1850 by architect S. V. Kerbedza and is the first permanent bridge across the Neva River. The bridge has a length of 331 meters and a width of 24 meters, and its weight is 95 thousand tons. The bridge consists of eight spans and seven shallow arches, one span is movable. All spans have the same flatness, which makes the bridge look harmonious. The bridge elements are connected by electric welding, and during construction, rivets and lead gaskets were used for joints.
