Aleksander Nevsky bridge

Alexander Nevsky Bridge is one of the most famous bridges in St. Petersburg. It is located across the Neva River and connects the central part of the city with the Nevsky Zastava district. Construction of the bridge began in 1960 and was completed in 1965. Alexander Nevsky Bridge is 629 meters long and 35 meters wide. It consists of five spans, the central span is movable. The adjustable mechanism allows you to extend the span for the passage of ships. The bridge is decorated with sculptures and bas-reliefs depicting scenes from the life of Alexander Nevsky. Four lanterns are installed on the bridge, each of which is made in the form of a rostral column with a lantern at the top. Alexander Nevsky Bridge is an important element of St. Petersburg's transport infrastructure. It provides a link between the central districts of the city and the Nevsky Outpost, and also serves to allow ships to pass through the Neva.
